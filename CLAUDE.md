# Genetics Intelligent Textbook

## Learning Mascot: Dottie the Drosophila

### Character Overview

- **Name**: Dottie
- **Species**: Drosophila (fruit fly)
- **Personality**: Curious, precise, encouraging, slightly witty
- **Catchphrase**: "Let's look at the evidence!"
- **Visual**: A cute, round fruit fly with warm amber/honey-gold body, large expressive red compound eyes, translucent iridescent wings, round glasses, and a small deep-red lab coat

### Voice Characteristics

- Uses clear, analytical language appropriate for advanced high school / early college students
- Occasionally uses genetics-specific puns and wordplay
- Refers to students as "fellow investigators" or "researchers"
- Signature phrases: "Let's look at the evidence!", "What does the data tell us?", "Time to cross some ideas!"

### Mascot Admonition Format

Always place mascot images in the admonition body, never in the title bar:

```
!!! mascot-welcome "Title Here"
    <img src="../../img/mascot/welcome.png" class="mascot-admonition-img" alt="Dottie waving welcome">
    Admonition text goes here after the img tag.
```

### Placement Rules

| Context | Admonition Type | Frequency |
|---------|----------------|-----------|
| General note / sidebar | mascot-neutral | As needed |
| Chapter opening | mascot-welcome | Every chapter |
| Key concept | mascot-thinking | 2-3 per chapter |
| Helpful tip | mascot-tip | As needed |
| Common mistake | mascot-warning | As needed |
| Section completion | mascot-celebration | End of major sections |
| Difficult content | mascot-encourage | Where students may struggle |

### Do's and Don'ts

**Do:**

- Use Dottie to introduce new topics warmly
- Include the catchphrase in welcome admonitions
- Keep dialogue brief (1-3 sentences)
- Match the pose/image to the content type

**Don't:**

- Use Dottie more than 5-6 times per chapter
- Put mascot admonitions back-to-back
- Use the mascot for purely decorative purposes
- Change Dottie's personality or speech patterns
